Why Hallucination is Inevitable

The Measure-Theoretic View of LLMs

An autoregressive model decomposes the joint distribution as a product of conditionals. The past token sequence \(X_{<t}\) generates a Borel σ-field \(\mathcal{F}_t = \sigma(x_1,\ldots,x_t)\), and each embedding vector becomes a random variable over a measurable space. The entire LLM is therefore a sequential approximator of conditional expectations — its output is not a pointwise truth value but average optimality over a probability distribution.

LP Space Norm \[ \|f\|_p = \left(\int_\Omega |f(\omega)|^p \, dP(\omega)\right)^{1/p} \]

The Minkowski inequality \(\|f+g\|_p \leq \|f\|_p + \|g\|_p\) enables semantic superposition (the source of generative flexibility), but combined with incompleteness, it also allows non-existent propositions to arise.

This fundamental mismatch makes LLMs well-suited for diverse text generation but inherently unsuited for inference requiring strict logical rigor — a critical failure mode in AI agents.

Core DFT Formulation

Sequence-Direction DFT \[ \hat{X}_k = \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} X_n \, e^{-2\pi i k n / N}, \quad \hat{X} = X F_N^T \in \mathbb{C}^{d \times N} \]
Amplitude–Phase Decomposition \[ \hat{X}_k = A_k \odot e^{i\Phi_k}, \quad A_k = |\hat{X}_k| \in \mathbb{R}^d, \quad \Phi_k = \angle\hat{X}_k \in \mathbb{R}^d \]
Frequency Mask (Semantic Operator) \[ \tilde{X} = F^{-1} M(\omega) F X, \quad M(k) = \begin{cases} 1.0 & k=0 \; \text{(preserve global topic)} \\ 0.5 & k=1,3 \; \text{(attenuate expression)} \\ 0.0 & k=2 \; \text{(remove sharp syntax)} \end{cases} \]

\(\hat{X}_L + \hat{X}'_H\) reconstructed via iDFT. LM head (linear + softmax) generates tokens

Theoretical Results

GPT-4's 1/f Spectral Structure

Natural language obeys Zipf's law and exhibits self-similar structure. The energy spectrum of embedding amplitudes decays as:

\[ E(f) \propto \frac{1}{f^\alpha}, \quad \alpha \approx 1.1 \sim 1.4 \]

The exponent exceeds 1 (pure 1/f) because syntactic constraints are more local than semantics, and LayerNorm/Attention regularizes high frequencies. If \(\alpha < 1\), grammar dominates but attention disperses; if \(\alpha > 2\), generation becomes monotonous and semantically shallow.

Hodge Decomposition & Harmonic Projection

On the graph Laplacian \(L = B^\top B\), the harmonic projection extracts globally consistent representations:

\[ \mathbb{R}^{mr} = \mathrm{im}(\delta^\dagger) \oplus \ker(L \otimes I_r) \oplus \mathrm{im}(\delta) \] \[ s_{\mathrm{harm}} = P_{\ker(L \otimes I_r)} \, s = U_0 U_0^\top s \]

The null space of the Laplacian (ker L) corresponds to harmonic 0-cochains — local representations that are mutually consistent across all windows. This projection does not eliminate genuine cohomological obstructions, but extracts the maximally consistent global semantic fragment.

06

Conclusion

This paper formalizes hallucination in large language models as a mathematically inevitable phenomenon arising from the structural mismatch between nonlinear truth sets and linear, convex embedding spaces — not from insufficient training or data defects.

The central contribution is demonstrating that dimensionality reduction from 24,576 → 3,000 dimensions is theoretically justified by cumulative energy analysis grounded in Zipf's law and the 1/f spectrum, while preserving semantic meaning and intent.

WavePhaseNet enables the semantic flow intent space → expression space → sentence generation to be written rigorously in linear algebraic form, opening a path toward next-generation reasoning-oriented language models with suppressed hallucination.
