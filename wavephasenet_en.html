01 // ABSTRACT

Overview

This paper reformulates the Transformer / Attention mechanism from the perspectives of measure theory and frequency analysis, theoretically clarifying why hallucination is structurally inevitable. The embedding space functions as a conditional expectation over a σ-additive family; its failure to be isomorphic to the semantic truth set is the fundamental cause of the breakdown of logical consistency.

Based on this observation, we propose WavePhaseNet — a method that explicitly constructs a Semantic Conceptual Hierarchy Structure (SCHS) within the embedding space using the Discrete Fourier Transform. Low-frequency components correspond to global meaning and intent; high-frequency components to local syntax and expression, enabling precise semantic manipulation in a diagonalized space.

02 // THEORY

The Structural Inevitability of Hallucination

Measure-Theoretic Formulation of Autoregressive Models

An autoregressive model decomposes the joint distribution as a product of conditionals. The past token sequence \(X_{<t}\) generates a Borel σ-field \(\mathcal{F}_t = \sigma(x_1,\ldots,x_t)\), and the embedding vector on the measurable space is interpretable as a random variable over this σ-additive family.

Conditional Expectation \[ \mathcal{L}^P(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, P) \ni f, \quad \|f\|_p = \left(\int_\Omega |f(\omega)|^p \, dP(\omega)\right)^{1/p} \]

The entire LLM is a sequential approximator of conditional expectations over σ-additive families. Its output is not a pointwise truth value but average optimality over a probability distribution (Lebesgue measure: expectation E).

Definition and Structural Inevitability of Hallucination

By the Minkowski inequality \(\|f+g\|_p \leq \|f\|_p + \|g\|_p\), different hypotheses can be linearly combined — a source of generative flexibility, but also of non-existent propositions arising from incompleteness.

Definition of Hallucination \[ \text{Truth set } T \subset V^*, \quad \text{Model-optimal set} \neq T \]

The truth set is generally non-convex and non-linear, while the embedding space has a linear and convex structure. This mismatch between a nonlinear truth set and a linear, convex representation space is the essential cause of hallucination — making LLMs fundamentally unsuited for inference requiring strict logical rigor.

Core DFT Formulation

Sequence-Direction DFT \[ \hat{X}_k = \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} x_n \, e^{-2\pi i k n / N}, \quad \hat{X} = X F_N^T \in \mathbb{C}^{d \times N} \]
Amplitude–Phase Decomposition \[ \hat{X}_k = A_k \odot e^{i\Phi_k}, \quad A_k = |\hat{X}_k| \in \mathbb{R}^d, \quad \Phi_k = \angle\hat{X}_k \in \mathbb{R}^d \]

Model Architecture

1
EMBEDDING LAYER
Token sequence → embedding matrix \(X \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times d}\) (sequence length N × embedding dimension d)
2
DFT LAYER (FFT)
FFT applied along the sequence direction → \(\hat{X} \in \mathbb{C}^{N \times d}\), computed in \(O(N \log N)\)
3
BAND SEPARATION
Multi-stage split: low-frequency set \(L = \{k : |k| \leq K_\text{low}\}\) separated from high-frequency components
4
LOW-FREQ MODULE (Intent)
Phase Φ and amplitude A encoded, frequency pooling yields global intent vector \(S \in \mathbb{R}^m\). Preserves positional sequence and mutual synchronization.
5
HIGH-FREQ MODULE (Generation)
Conditional generator \(G\) outputs \(\hat{X}'_H \sim p(\hat{X}_H | S)\). Options include normalizing flows, conditional AR models, or VAE / diffusion models.
6
INVERSE DFT
\(\hat{X}_L + \hat{X}'_H\) reconstructed via iDFT. Lightweight implementation via PyTorch torch.fft.fft / ifft.

04 // RESULTS

Theoretical Results

1/f Spectral Structure of GPT-4 (24,576 Dimensions)

Natural language follows Zipf's law and exhibits self-similar structure. The energy spectrum of embedding amplitudes shows a 1/f-type decay:

\[ E(f) \propto \frac{1}{f^\alpha}, \quad \alpha \approx 1.1 \sim 1.4 \]

The exponent α > 1 arises because syntactic constraints are more local than semantics, and LayerNorm / Attention regularize high frequencies. If α < 1 grammar dominates but attention disperses; if α > 2 generation becomes semantically shallow.

WavePhaseNet vs. FNet
FNet discards the imaginary part of the complex representation, weakening handling of coreference and contextual dependency. WavePhaseNet retains phase information and introduces explicit semantic hierarchy operations on frequency bands — a fundamentally distinct contribution.
Beyond RAG
Comparison with RAG-Based Approaches
RAG introduces strong external dependencies and poor extensibility. WavePhaseNet constructs SCHS directly within the embedding space — no external knowledge store required — significantly reducing the need for advanced prompt engineering.
06 // CONCLUSION

Conclusion

This paper formalizes hallucination in large language models as a mathematically inevitable phenomenon arising from structural mismatch, and presents a theoretical framework to suppress it by introducing a DFT-based SCHS into the embedding space. The demonstration that reduction from 24,576 to ~3,000 dimensions is justified by cumulative energy analysis grounded in Zipf's law and the 1/f spectrum constitutes the core contribution.

WavePhaseNet enables semantic flow — intent space → expression space → sentence generation — to be described rigorously in linear algebraic form, opening a path toward next-generation reasoning-oriented language models.
