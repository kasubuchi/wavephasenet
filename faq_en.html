Theoretical Foundations
What is the basis for the assumption "low frequency = semantics, high frequency = syntax"?

The DC component ($k=0$) in the DFT is the sequence-wise average, contributing uniformly to all token positions. It is mathematically guaranteed to represent global features of the entire sentence — intent, summary, and topic.

This correspondence has been empirically validated in prior work, notably FNet (arXiv), which demonstrated that replacing the Attention mechanism with Fourier transforms achieves acceleration while preserving semantic analysis. However, FNet discarded the imaginary part of the complex representation, resulting in weakness in handling coreference relations and contextual dependencies. WavePhaseNet differs fundamentally by preserving phase information and explicitly introducing operations on semantic hierarchies.

Isn't the logical leap from the Minkowski inequality to hallucination problematic?

The paper does not claim that "hallucination arises from the Minkowski inequality." The Minkowski inequality guarantees norm stability in $L^p$ spaces ($\|f+g\|_p \leq \|f\|_p + \|g\|_p$), showing that different semantic components can be linearly combined in the embedding space while maintaining stability. This is the source of generative AI's flexibility.

The cause of hallucination lies in the fact that LLM's semantic space is deformed at each Transformer layer, locally distorted by Attention, and dynamically reconstructed by context — exhibiting the characteristics of an incomplete space: non-convergence, non-existence of limits, unstable distances. These properties constitute the structural basis for the inevitability of hallucination.

Dimensionality Reduction
Is the derivation of 3,000 dimensions rigorous?

The value of 3,000 dimensions is not arbitrarily chosen. It is derived through the following theoretical process:

By Zipf's law, frequency distributions follow $f(r) \propto r^{-\alpha}$. Through the Yule–Simon process, this converges to $f(r) \sim r^{-1}$ in the limit. The scale invariance yields an energy distribution $A_k^2 \propto k^{-\alpha}$. For structured embeddings in GPT-4 class models, $\alpha \in [1.1, 1.4]$, and evaluating the cumulative energy $\sum_{k=1}^{K} k^{-\alpha}$ shows that $K = 3072$ reaches 82–88% — the threshold for preserving meaning and intent.

This is a theoretical derivation based on Zipf's law and scale invariance, not trial-and-error estimation. Herein lies the theoretical value of this paper.

How is $\alpha \in [1.1, 1.4]$ derived?

The spectrum deviates from a perfect $1/f$ ($\alpha = 1$) due to: (1) the existence of syntactic constraints, (2) the locality of grammar relative to semantics, and (3) intrinsic high-frequency regularization by Transformers (LayerNorm, etc.). These cause high frequencies to decay more strongly, pushing $\alpha > 1$. Meanwhile, the preservation of stylistic diversity constrains $\alpha \leq 1.4$.

Specifically, applying DFT along the token sequence direction, averaging over embedding dimensions, and plotting $\log A_k^2$ vs. $\log k$ yields an approximately linear relationship with slope in the range $-1.1$ to $-1.4$.

Cohomological Regularization
How does cohomological regularization differ from standard graph Laplacian regularization?

Standard graph regularization enforces smoothness between adjacent nodes. Our cohomological regularization integrates three elements: (1) coboundary energy quantifying inconsistency between local inferences, (2) Hodge decomposition for harmonic projection (extraction of contradiction-free components), and (3) consistency constraints with the DFT-derived global intent $G$.

The essential difference lies in leveraging Hodge theory to treat the gluing of local inferences topologically and algebraically, ensuring the validity of "reasoning aligned with global meaning" through Čech cohomology connections.

What is the cohomological definition of hallucination?

Hallucination is defined as a "nontrivial first cohomology class":

$$H^1(X, \mathcal{K}) \neq 0 \iff \text{Locally consistent but globally non-glueable inferences exist}$$

When local sections of the knowledge sheaf $\mathcal{K}$ fail to agree on overlap regions, no global section (consistent reasoning result) exists. This topological inconsistency is the mathematical substance of hallucination.

Empirical Validation
Isn't the absence of experimental results a problem?

This paper is positioned as a mathematical theory paper. The research process follows the paradigm of first establishing a theoretical framework, then verifying it through experiments. The perfect reconstructability of DFT has been demonstrated through numerical examples, and the theoretical validity of semantic operations via frequency masks has been shown.

Future work includes quantitative evaluation of hallucination suppression using benchmarks such as TruthfulQA, spectral analysis of actual GPT embeddings, and performance retention verification under 3,000-dimensional reduction.

Research Significance
Does WavePhaseNet go beyond Transformers?

The Attention mechanism computes position-dependent nonlinear weighted sums, mixing global and local semantics. WavePhaseNet's frequency-based semantic operations enable the semantic flow "intent space → expression space → sentence generation" to be rigorously described in linear algebraic form.

This opens the possibility of achieving mathematically principled control over complex reasoning tasks that currently depend on prompt engineering. Rather than "replacing" Transformers, the proposed approach integrates by injecting a Spectral Module and Cohomology Regularizer into each layer and each head.

What is the most significant contribution of this theory?

The most significant contribution is the formulation: "Reasoning is a global section search problem over the knowledge sheaf." It defines correctness of reasoning not as "path validity" but as "glueability," enabling mathematical detection of hallucination as "topological inconsistency."

By connecting three mathematical disciplines — measure theory, spectral analysis, and cohomology — and attempting a structural understanding of the essence of LLMs, this work aims to serve as an innovative theoretical bridge toward AGI/ASI.
